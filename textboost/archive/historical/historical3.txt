The Code of Hammurabi (c. 1754 BCE) - One of the earliest known legal codes in history, the Code of Hammurabi was established by the Babylonian king Hammurabi around 1754 BCE. Carved on a large stone monument, it comprised 282 laws that governed various aspects of Babylonian society. The code followed the principle of "an eye for an eye" and sought to bring about justice and maintain social order.
