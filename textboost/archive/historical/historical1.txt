The Declaration of Independence (1776) - The Declaration of Independence, a seminal document in American history, was adopted on July 4, 1776. It proclaimed the thirteen American colonies' independence from British rule and laid the foundation for the formation of the United States of America. Written primarily by Thomas Jefferson, this influential document outlined the principles of individual rights, self-governance, and equality, asserting that "all men are created equal" and have the unalienable rights of "life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness."
