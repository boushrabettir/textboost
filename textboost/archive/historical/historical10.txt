The Epic of Gilgamesh (c. 18th century BCE) - An ancient Mesopotamian epic poem, the Epic of Gilgamesh is regarded as one of the earliest surviving works of literature. It follows the adventures of the Sumerian king Gilgamesh and his friend Enkidu as they seek immortality and confront the inevitability of mortality. The poem explores themes of friendship, mortality, and the quest for wisdom, resonating with universal human experiences.
