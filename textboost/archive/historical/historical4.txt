The Gettysburg Address (1863) - Delivered by President Abraham Lincoln during the American Civil War, the Gettysburg Address is one of the most famous speeches in American history. Given on November 19, 1863, at the dedication of the Soldiers' National Cemetery in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania, the speech emphasized the principles of equality and liberty, honoring the sacrifice of soldiers while calling for a renewed commitment to the ideals of the Declaration of Independence.
