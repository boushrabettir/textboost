The Iliad by Homer (c. 8th century BCE) - One of the oldest surviving works of Western literature, the Iliad is an epic poem attributed to the ancient Greek poet Homer. Set during the Trojan War, the poem recounts the events surrounding the quarrel between the Greek hero Achilles and King Agamemnon, culminating in the fall of the city of Troy. The Iliad is revered for its depiction of heroic ideals, divine intervention, and the consequences of pride and wrath.
