The Constitution of the United States (1787) - The Constitution of the United States, drafted during the Constitutional Convention in 1787 and ratified in 1788, is the supreme law of the United States. It established the framework for the federal government and its three branches: the legislative, executive, and judicial branches. The Constitution is characterized by its flexibility and enduring relevance, as it provides a blueprint for governance that has adapted to the changing needs of the nation over the centuries.
